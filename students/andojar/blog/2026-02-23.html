The Paradox of Choice

Mondays are for quizzes—except when they aren't. To my surprise, we didn't have one today, which meant I suddenly had a surplus of free time on my hands. But instead of jumping into my projects, I found myself in a bit of a procrastinative lull.

I’ve noticed a pattern lately: when I feel truly "work-free," my momentum tends to flatline temporarily. I end up thinking about *how* I’m going to work, what I’m going to build, or even what my next big move should be, rather than just doing it. The "thinking before working" phase can be a double-edged sword—valuable for planning, but frustrating when it doesn't lead to a single commit.

Still, sometimes the mind just needs that extra bit of space to roam before it can buckle down again. It's a reminder to keep the momentum going, even when the pressure isn't on.
