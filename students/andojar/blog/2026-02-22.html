Parkinson's Law

Sundays are usually for resetting, but today felt more like an accumulation of everything. My mind was busy—honestly, a bit too busy. I spent most of the day just trying to organize my life, sorting through "pendings" and reviewing lessons for a supposed Monday quiz.

The mental load was real. When my mind gets like this, it feels like I’m working at 100% capacity just on background processes. It was one of those days where I did a lot of thinking but didn't actually commit any code. Sometimes the fear of making a mistake when you're mentally exhausted is just too high.

I realized today that I tend to struggle with progressive commits when my mind is this cluttered. It’s hard to break things down when everything feels like one giant problem. For now, I've just cleared the mental list for tomorrow. No pressure, just survival.
